How Much Do You Need to Retire in India?

The 25× Rule is a popular retirement planning guideline: multiply your annual retirement expenses by 25 to get your target corpus. This ensures you can withdraw 4% per year indefinitely (the "4% safe withdrawal rate").

Rule of 25 Example

If you need ₹60,000/month at retirement = ₹7,20,000/year × 25 = ₹1.8 Crore corpus needed (at today's value). Adjusted for inflation at 6% over 30 years, you'd need ~₹10.3 Crore at retirement age.

Best Retirement Investment Options India

  • NPS (National Pension System): Additional ₹50,000 deduction u/s 80CCD(1B), partial tax-free withdrawals
  • EPF/VPF: Employer + employee contributions, 8.25% interest (tax-free)
  • Equity Mutual Funds (SIP): Historical 12-15% CAGR over long term
  • PPF: 7.1% tax-free, 15-year compounding, max ₹1.5L/year
