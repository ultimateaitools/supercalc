Understanding Sleep Cycles

The human sleep cycle lasts approximately 90 minutes. Each cycle consists of 4 stages: N1 (light sleep), N2 (intermediate sleep), N3 (deep sleep/slow-wave), and REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep.

Why Wake at End of a Cycle?

Waking mid-cycle (especially during deep sleep N3) causes sleep inertia — that groggy, disoriented feeling. Waking at the end of a 90-minute cycle means you're in lighter sleep, making it much easier to feel alert immediately.
