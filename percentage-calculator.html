Percentage Formulas

P% of X = (P / 100) × X

X is what % of Y = (X / Y) × 100

% Change = ((New - Old) / Old) × 100

Original = Final / (1 + P/100) [after % increase]
Original = Final / (1 - P/100) [after % decrease]

Frequently Asked Questions

To calculate percentage: Divide the part by the whole, then multiply by 100. Example: If you scored 45 out of 60, your percentage = (45/60) × 100 = 75%.
% Increase = ((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) × 100. Example: Price increased from ₹200 to ₹250. % Increase = ((250-200)/200) × 100 = 25%.
20% of 500 = (20/100) × 500 = 0.20 × 500 = 100. So 20% of 500 is 100.
