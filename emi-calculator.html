What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both the principal amount and interest each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is fully repaid.

EMI Formula

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ / ((1 + r)ⁿ – 1)

Where:
P = Principal loan amount
r = Monthly interest rate (Annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
n = Number of monthly installments

How to Use This EMI Calculator

  3. Enter the Loan Tenure in years or months

Tips to Reduce Your EMI

  • Make a larger down payment to reduce principal
  • Choose a longer tenure (note: increases total interest)
  • Negotiate a lower interest rate with your bank
  • Make prepayments whenever possible
  • Maintain a good credit score (750+) for better rates
