🔄 Compound vs Simple Interest

Compound Interest Formula

A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t)

CI = A - P

Where:
A = Final amount
P = Principal amount
r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
n = Compounding frequency per year
t = Time in years

Simple Interest vs Compound Interest

Simple Interest: SI = P × r × t (interest on principal only). Compound Interest grows much faster because interest earns interest. The difference becomes dramatic over long periods — this is the "power of compounding".

Effect of Compounding Frequency

For ₹1,00,000 at 12% for 10 years:

  • Annually: ₹3,10,585
  • Quarterly: ₹3,26,204
  • Monthly: ₹3,30,039
  • Daily: ₹3,31,946

More frequent compounding = more wealth creation, though the difference narrows at higher frequencies.

FAQ

Rule of 72: Divide 72 by the interest rate to find how many years it takes to double your money. At 12%, money doubles in 72/12 = 6 years. At 8%, it takes 9 years. It's a quick mental math shortcut for compound interest.
As an investor, compound interest is always better — your money grows faster. As a borrower, simple interest is better — you pay less. Banks use compound interest for savings accounts and loans, making compound interest knowledge essential for financial planning.
