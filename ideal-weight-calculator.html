Ideal Weight Formulas Explained

Robinson (1983): Men = 52 + 1.9kg/inch above 5ft | Women = 49 + 1.7kg/inch above 5ft
Miller (1983): Men = 56.2 + 1.41kg/inch above 5ft | Women = 53.1 + 1.36kg/inch above 5ft
Devine (1974): Men = 50 + 2.3kg/inch above 5ft | Women = 45.5 + 2.3kg/inch above 5ft

No single formula is perfect — your ideal weight depends on muscle mass, age, activity level, and body composition. BMI range (18.5-24.9) is the most widely used healthy weight indicator, but elite athletes may have higher BMI due to muscle mass.
