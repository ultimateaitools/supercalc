What is SIP?

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly (monthly/quarterly) in mutual funds. It helps in rupee cost averaging and builds wealth through the power of compounding over time.

SIP Formula

FV = P × {[(1 + r)ⁿ – 1] / r} × (1 + r)

Where:
FV = Future value / Maturity amount
P = Monthly investment amount
r = Monthly rate of return (Annual return ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
n = Total number of payments (Years × 12)

Benefits of SIP Investment

  • Rupee Cost Averaging — Buy more units when price is low, fewer when high
  • Power of Compounding — Returns earn returns, multiplying wealth over time
  • Disciplined Investing — Regular investment habit regardless of market conditions
  • Flexibility — Start with as low as ₹500/month
  • Liquidity — Redeem anytime (except ELSS 3-year lock-in)

Frequently Asked Questions

Historically, equity mutual funds in India have delivered 12-15% CAGR over the long term. For conservative estimates, use 10-12%. Debt funds typically give 6-8%. ELSS funds average 12-14%.
Step-Up SIP (or Top-Up SIP) allows you to increase your SIP amount by a fixed percentage each year. For example, if you start with ₹5,000/month and increase by 10% annually, after 1 year you invest ₹5,500/month. This helps combat inflation and accelerates wealth creation.
SIP is better for regular investors as it reduces timing risk through rupee cost averaging. Lump sum is better if you have a large amount and can invest when markets are low. For most investors, SIP is recommended as it builds discipline and reduces risk.
SIP invests in mutual funds (market-linked, higher risk, higher potential returns), while FD offers fixed returns with capital protection. SIP historically gives 12-15% vs FD's 6-7%, but SIP returns are not guaranteed. For long-term wealth creation, SIP generally outperforms FD after tax.
