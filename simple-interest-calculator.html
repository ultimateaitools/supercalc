Simple Interest Formula

SI = P × R × T / 100
A = P + SI = P(1 + RT/100)

Where: P = Principal, R = Rate%, T = Time (years)

Examples

  • ₹10,000 at 10% for 2 years → SI = 10000×10×2/100 = ₹2,000, Amount = ₹12,000
  • ₹50,000 at 8% for 18 months → T = 1.5 years → SI = 50000×8×1.5/100 = ₹6,000

SI vs Compound Interest

Simple interest is calculated on the original principal only. Compound interest calculates on principal + accumulated interest, making it grow faster. Banks typically use compound interest for loans and deposits.
