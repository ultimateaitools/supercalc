What is TDEE?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories your body burns in a day, taking into account your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) and physical activity level. Knowing your TDEE is the foundation of any diet or fitness plan.

Calorie Formula (Mifflin-St Jeor)

BMR (Men) = 10W + 6.25H - 5A + 5
BMR (Women) = 10W + 6.25H - 5A - 161

TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier

Where: W = weight (kg), H = height (cm), A = age (years)

Activity Multipliers

  • Sedentary (1.2) — Desk job, little exercise
  • Light (1.375) — Exercise 1-3 days/week
  • Moderate (1.55) — Exercise 3-5 days/week
  • Very Active (1.725) — Hard exercise 6-7 days/week
  • Extra Active (1.9) — Athlete or physical labor job

Frequently Asked Questions

To lose weight safely, create a calorie deficit of 300-500 calories per day. This leads to approximately 0.3-0.5 kg weight loss per week. Never go below 1,200 calories/day (women) or 1,500 calories/day (men) without medical supervision.
BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the calories your body burns at complete rest — just to keep organs functioning. TDEE includes BMR plus all calories burned through physical activity. TDEE is what you should base your diet on.
1 kg of body fat contains approximately 7,700 calories. To lose 1 kg/week, you need a calorie deficit of about 1,100 calories/day. A more sustainable rate is 0.5 kg/week (deficit of ~550 calories/day).
