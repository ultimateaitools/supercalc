What is a Recurring Deposit (RD)?

A Recurring Deposit (RD) is a savings scheme where you deposit a fixed amount every month for a specific tenure. At maturity, you receive the total amount deposited plus the interest earned. RD is ideal for regular savers as it inculcates a habit of saving regularly.

RD Maturity Formula

M = R × [(1+i)^n – 1] / (1 – (1+i)^(-1/3))

Where: M = Maturity amount, R = Monthly installment, i = Rate of interest/400, n = Tenure in quarters

Key Benefits of RD

  • Start with as low as ₹100/month (Post Office) or ₹500/month (most banks)
  • Deposits insured up to ₹5 lakh per bank under DICGC
  • Loan facility available against RD (up to 90% of deposit value)
  • Senior citizens get 0.25%-0.50% extra interest rate

RD vs SIP — Which is Better?

RD guarantees returns (currently 6.5-7.5%) while SIP in equity mutual funds has historically delivered 12-15% CAGR over 5+ years. For risk-free guaranteed returns, choose RD. For wealth creation over the long term, SIP is better.
